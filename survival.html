Survival of the Fittest
Illustration

Survival of the Fittest is a comic book based off the biblical story The Parable of the Good Samaritan (Luke 10:25-37). The similar concept between the two is the need for a character to overcome social boundaries in order to help another in need.

The Design

To keep the colour scheme simple while keeping the style dramatic, a complimentary colour scheme of yellow and purple was used.

In this comic, the yellow represents warmth and comfort, while the purple represents loneliness or emptiness. When the main character has a flashback, it is dipped in a desaturated red tone, which continues the idea of warmth, but then quickly turns into aggression.

The black lines and misshapen panels also add to the comicâ€™s sense of drama.

The Process

The characters were designed to have distinctive differences: one being the stereotypical definition of strong, and one bearing emotional strength.

Thus, the father physically appears strong and wields a large sword (a weapon used in the thick of battle), while the son is much smaller in stature and only wields a bow and arrow (a weapon used further away from the action).
