CanadaBuys

Visual Design

The CanadaBuys visual design is the look and feel of CanadaBuys, the new website for doing business with the Government. Characteristic to the design are five “ambassadors”, which not only have symbolic meaning, but lead the user throughout different portions of the site.

Visit CanadaBuys

The Design

The aim of the visual design was to push Government web pages in a new and innovative direction, while also being meaningful to CanadaBuys. It is built off CanadaBuys’ core values and goals:



  • Economic growth

  • Modernization

  • Simplicity

  • Green Procurement



The idea of money best represented economic growth and from this, the CanadaBuys ambassadors were born. Each ambassador was taken from the imagery on the Canadian coins, while the colour scheme references Canadian bills.



The overall geometric style encompassed the two values of modernization and simplicity, and the design was brought together by placing the ambassadors in natural environments – an ode to green procurement.



The full description of the visual design can be found on CanadaBuys.



When the design is applied to the site, it also serves a functional purpose. Each section of the site has been themed with an ambassador and a colour, enhancing the user’s ability to understand where they are on any given page. Since all of the visual design imagery on CanadaBuys is mainly decorative in that regard, the images do not require alt text, which simplifies the experience for those view the site with screen readers. Additionally, this imagery is made up of SVG images, which decreases load times.

The Process

The beginnings of the visual design began with brainstorming sessions within the User Experience and Service Design team at PSPC. Guided by my supervisor, I led these discussions and we all contributed ideas on all things Canadian. The design started with a Canadian map, then the idea of connected dots to better represent business and commerce. However, the beavers drawn on the whiteboard from our brainstorming sessions led us to the ambassadors seen today.



After settling on the concept of using the animals from the Canadian coins, we ran with the idea. I mocked up the CanadaBuys homepage to begin fleshing out the rest of the visual design. Once we came to a solid understanding of the CanadaBuys look and feel, I documented everything in a detailed styleguide.
